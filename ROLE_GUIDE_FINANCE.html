💰 Finance Controller Guide

Valuation & Ledgering

Welcome to Kalike Asset Management

As a Finance Controller, you manage asset valuations, depreciation calculations, financial reporting, and ledger entries. Your focus is on the financial aspects of asset management—tracking cost, depreciation, and building financial reports.

Your Dashboard Overview

💹 Finance Dashboard shows:
  • Total asset value (acquisition + current)
  • Depreciation trends and schedules
  • Grant allocations and tracking
  • Asset ledger (all transactions)
  • Depreciation reports
  • Financial KPIs and metrics

Core Features

1️⃣ Asset Ledger Management

Complete Asset Valuation & Ledger:
  • View all assets with financial data
  • Track acquisition cost and date
  • Monitor current valuation
  • View asset depreciation schedule
  • Generate ledger reports for accounting

2️⃣ Depreciation Management

Depreciation Calculations & Tracking:
  • Automatic depreciation calculations (straight-line, declining balance, etc.)
  • Configure depreciation policies by asset category
  • View depreciation schedule for each asset
  • Generate depreciation reports for financial statements
  • Track accumulated depreciation
💡 Depreciation Methods Supported: Straight-line, Declining Balance, Units of Production, Sum-of-Years-Digits

3️⃣ Grant Management

Track assets funded through grants:

  • Record grant allocations and amounts
  • Link assets to specific grants
  • Track grant usage and compliance
  • Generate grant reports for audits
  • Monitor grant-funded asset depreciation separately

4️⃣ Financial Reports

Generate Reports:
  • 📋 Asset register (full inventory with values)
  • 📋 Depreciation schedules by category
  • 📋 Fixed asset report (for balance sheet)
  • 📋 Grant compliance report
  • 📋 Asset disposal/write-off report
  • 📋 Year-over-year valuation trends

5️⃣ Asset Valuation

Financial analysis of assets:

  • Gross value (acquisition cost)
  • Accumulated depreciation
  • Net book value (current value)
  • Market value (current market price)
  • Impairment tracking

6️⃣ Disposal & Write-Off

Handle asset end-of-life:

  • Record asset disposals
  • Calculate gain/loss on sale
  • Track write-offs
  • Generate disposal reports for tax purposes

Key Financial Metrics

Metric Meaning Used For
Acquisition Cost Price paid when asset was purchased Balance sheet valuation
Accumulated Depreciation Total depreciation since purchase Calculate net book value
Net Book Value Acquisition Cost - Accumulated Depreciation Financial reporting
Depreciation Expense Expense recognized this period Income statement
Salvage Value Estimated value at end of useful life Depreciation calculations
Useful Life Expected years of productive use Depreciation period

Depreciation Policy Configuration

You can set depreciation policies by asset category:

  • 💻 IT Equipment: 3-5 year useful life, straight-line
  • 🪑 Furniture: 7-10 year useful life, straight-line
  • 🛠️ Heavy Equipment: 5-10 year useful life, declining balance
  • 📞 Communication Devices: 3-4 year useful life, straight-line

Common Tasks

Task: Month-End Asset Valuation

  1. Run depreciation recalculation
  2. Review new asset acquisitions
  3. Check for impairments or write-offs
  4. Generate balance sheet report
  5. Reconcile to general ledger

Task: Annual Depreciation Review

  1. Review useful life estimates (still accurate?)
  2. Adjust depreciation policies if needed
  3. Generate annual depreciation report
  4. Prepare tax depreciation schedules
  5. Document any changes for audit

Task: Grant Compliance Report

  1. Filter assets by grant
  2. Verify all grant amounts accounted for
  3. Check usage within restrictions
  4. Generate compliance report
  5. Submit to grant administrator

Task: Asset Disposal

  1. Mark asset as "disposed"
  2. Enter disposal date and price (if sold)
  3. System calculates gain/loss
  4. Generate disposal report for tax
  5. Remove from active inventory

Frequently Asked Questions

Q: How is depreciation calculated?

A: Straight-line is most common: (Cost - Salvage Value) / Useful Life = Annual Depreciation

Q: Can I change depreciation method mid-year?

A: Yes, but it's an accounting change that should be documented. Consult your auditor.

Q: How do I handle grants for asset purchases?

A: Record the grant allocation, link assets to it, and track usage for compliance reporting.

Q: What happens when an asset is impaired?

A: Mark the asset condition, finance system calculates impairment, generates adjustment entry.

Q: Can I export data for external reporting?

A: Yes, generate reports in Excel/PDF formats for auditors and financial statements.
